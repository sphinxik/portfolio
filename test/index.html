Alessandro Veratti
Chef

Alessandro Veratti was born on April 12, 1982, in the picturesque town of Siena, located in the heart of Tuscany, Italy. From a young age, Alessandro displayed a passion for cooking, growing up amid the meadows, vineyards, and olive groves that later became the foundation of his culinary expertise.

After completing his schooling in Siena, he enrolled in the prestigious Apicius Culinary Academy in Florence, where he mastered the basics of Italian cuisine, honed his skills, and studied the subtleties of flavor pairing. During his studies, Alessandro undertook internships at several renowned restaurants in Florence, where he had the opportunity to work with some of Italy’s most respected chefs.

He graduated with honors from the academy in 2003 and decided to further his education by enrolling at the University of Gastronomic Sciences in Piedmont, which is considered one of the best in the world for culinary arts and gastronomy.

During his time at the university, which he completed in 2006 with a Master’s degree in Gastronomic Sciences, Alessandro focused on learning modern culinary techniques and explored the impact of global cuisine on traditional Italian dishes.
